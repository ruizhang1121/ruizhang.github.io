About me

I am a third-year Ph.D. student at School of ECE, Georgia Tech. I'm very fortunate to be advised by Prof. Gee-Kung Chang. Prior to Georgia Tech, I received B.S. in Electrical engineering and B.A. in Economics from Peking University in 2017, where I was fortunate to work with Prof. Zhiping(James) Zhou. In summer 2016, I worked with Prof. S.J.Ben Yoo at UC Davis. In 2018 and 2019, I spent wonderful summers as a research intern with Dr. Winston Way at Neophotonics.

Research

My research interests are broadly in coherent optical communication systems, high-performance digital signal processing techniques for 5G and beyond, free space optical transmission, millimeter wave transmission, and passive optical networks. Currently, I'm particularly interested in the following topics:

  • Laser Phase noise characterization and DSP techniques in coherent systems.

  • Probabilistic shaping based system optimization.

  • High-performance millimeter wave and free space optics communication.
